Borscht

Ingredients

  • Beef - 500 g
  • Beet - 1
  • Potatoes - 2
  • White cabbage - 200 g
  • Carrot - 1
  • Onion - 1
  • Tomato paste - 1 tablespoon
  • Vegetable oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Vinegar - 1 teaspoon
  • Bay leaf - 1
  • Black peppercorns - 2–3
  • Salt - 2 teaspoons (to taste)
  • Water - 1.5 liters

Steps

  1. Cut the beef into large pieces.
  2. Cover the meat in a pot with cold water, bring to a boil, and skim off the foam. Simmer the broth for about 1.5 hours over low heat.
  3. Peel the beets and cut them into strips.
  4. Peel the carrots and grate them using a coarse grater.
  5. Peel the onion and chop it finely.
  6. Chop the cabbage.
  7. Peel the potatoes and cut them into cubes.
  8. Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet. Add the beets, carrots, and onion to the skillet and sauté over medium heat, stirring occasionally, for 5–7 minutes.
  9. At the end, add the vinegar and tomato paste. Stir. Cook the vegetables for another 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  10. Add the potatoes and cabbage to the boiling broth and cook for 10 minutes. (Add the young cabbage 5 minutes before the borscht is done.)
  11. Then add the sautéed vegetables, bay leaf, and pepper. Cook the beef borscht for another 5–7 minutes.
  12. Let the finished borscht sit for 10–15 minutes. Chop the herbs.
